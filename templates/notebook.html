The Science of Climate Change

The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that keeps Earth habitable. However, human activities are artificially amplifying it. When sunlight reaches Earth, the surface absorbs it and re-emits it as heat (infrared radiation). Greenhouse gases — CO₂, methane, nitrous oxide, and water vapor — trap this heat, preventing it from escaping into space.

Key Fact: Without any greenhouse effect, Earth's average temperature would be -18°C instead of +15°C. The problem is the enhanced greenhouse effect from human emissions.

Climate Feedback Loops

  • Ice-Albedo Feedback: As ice melts, dark ocean/land is exposed, absorbing more heat, melting more ice — a self-reinforcing cycle
  • Permafrost Feedback: Thawing permafrost releases stored methane and CO₂, further warming the planet
  • Water Vapor Feedback: Warmer air holds more water vapor (a GHG), amplifying initial warming
  • Forest Dieback: Warmer, drier conditions kill forests, turning them from carbon sinks to carbon sources

Effects of Climate Change

🌡️ Temperature Rise

Earth has warmed +1.45°C since pre-industrial times. Every fraction of a degree matters — the difference between 1.5°C and 2°C means 70% more coral reef bleaching, 420 million more people exposed to heatwaves, and 2× more flooding.

🧊 Polar Ice Loss

Arctic is warming 4× faster than the global average. Summer sea ice could disappear before 2035. Greenland lost 3.3 trillion tonnes of ice since 2002. If fully melted: +7 metres sea level rise globally.

🌊 Sea Level Rise

Rising 3.7mm/year — 3× faster than in 1993. 800 million people live in low-lying coastal areas at risk. Miami, Jakarta, Mumbai, Shanghai — all facing existential flooding threats by 2100.

🔥 Extreme Weather

Heatwaves are 5× more likely. Rainfall events are 40% more intense. Wildfire seasons have extended by 3 months globally. Hurricane maximum wind speeds are intensifying as ocean temperatures rise.

🌊 Ocean Acidification

Oceans absorb 25% of CO₂, making them 26% more acidic than pre-industrial. Coral reefs are bleaching globally. Shell-forming organisms are dissolving. The entire marine food chain is at risk.

🦋 Biodiversity Loss

1 million species face extinction. 60% of wildlife populations lost since 1970. Climate change is the 2nd largest driver after habitat destruction. Ecosystem collapse threatens our own food security.

🍽️ Food Security

Crop yields declining 2–6% per decade due to heat and drought. 800 million people already face hunger. Climate change could push 130 million more into extreme poverty by 2030.

🏥 Human Health

Air pollution from fossil fuels causes 7 million premature deaths/year. Heat-related deaths are increasing. Disease vectors (malaria, dengue) are expanding into new regions as temperatures rise.

Solutions & What We Must Do

Energy Transition

  • Solar is now the cheapest electricity in history (<$0.02/kWh in optimal locations). Capacity doubled in 2023 alone.
  • Wind energy (onshore + offshore) can power all of Europe. 15MW offshore turbines are now commercial.
  • Nuclear provides 10% of global electricity with near-zero emissions. Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) are next-generation.
  • Green hydrogen (from electrolysis + renewables) can decarbonize shipping, aviation, and heavy industry.
  • Grid-scale batteries make renewable intermittency manageable. Costs fell 97% since 1991.

Transport Decarbonization

  • Electric vehicles now outsell petrol cars in many markets. EVs emit 50–70% less CO₂ over their lifetime.
  • High-speed rail can replace short-haul flights (under 600km) completely.
  • Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) can cut aviation lifecycle emissions by 80%.
  • Ammonia and methanol-powered ships are commercially available now.

Nature-Based Solutions

  • Planting 1 trillion trees could sequester 205 gigatonnes of CO₂ over decades.
  • Protecting existing forests is 3–5× more effective than planting new ones.
  • Restoring peatlands, wetlands, and mangroves provides carbon storage and coastal protection.
  • Regenerative agriculture sequesters carbon in soil while improving yields.

Policy & Economics

  • Carbon pricing makes polluting expensive. Sweden's $130/tonne tax cut emissions 27% while economy grew 100%.
  • The EU's Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism levels the playing field globally.
  • The Inflation Reduction Act ($369B) is the largest climate investment in US history.
  • Green bonds market hit $500 billion in 2023. Finance has the scale to accelerate transition.
Key Message: Every solution needed already exists. The challenge is speed and political will — not technology. AI like this platform accelerates both.

Future Scenarios

BEST CASE: +1.5°C
Requires emergency action starting now. Deep emission cuts across all sectors by 2030. Possible with current technology but needs unprecedented political will. Coral reefs stressed but survive. Managed coastal flooding. Nature recovers over time.
MIDDLE CASE: +2.0°C
The Paris Agreement's upper limit. Coral reefs largely destroyed. Significant ice loss. Hundreds of millions face food and water stress. Higher flood frequency. Still manageable with massive adaptation investment.
LIKELY CASE: +2.7°C
Current trajectory with existing pledges (but without full implementation). Severe disruption to food systems, coastal cities, and ecosystems. Mass migration. Major economic disruption. Large parts of tropics become difficult to inhabit.
WORST CASE: +4°C+
Business-as-usual with no new action. Catastrophic and irreversible changes. Potential collapse of agricultural systems. Uninhabitable tropics. Billions displaced. Feedback loops may trigger runaway warming beyond human control.
